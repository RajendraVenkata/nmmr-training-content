# L03: Rapid Prototyping & Validation

## Overview

Learn how to iterate quickly on AI solutions, building MVPs that validate business value before committing to full implementation. This lesson covers fast iteration techniques, user feedback loops, and the discipline of validating AI solutions with real users rather than assumptions.

## Topics Covered

- Fast iteration on AI solutions using agile techniques
- MVP development for AI applications
- User feedback loops for validating AI value
- Validating AI solutions with real users before full investment
- Knowing when to pivot, persevere, or stop
- Transitioning successful prototypes to production systems

## Lab: Rapidly Prototyping an AI Solution from Business Requirement to Working Demo

Take a business requirement, design an AI solution, build a working prototype, and validate it with simulated user feedback, all within a compressed timeline that mirrors real-world expectations.

### Lab Objectives

- Translate a business requirement into an AI solution design within a constrained timeframe
- Build a working prototype using rapid development tools (Streamlit, Gradio, or Jupyter)
- Gather and incorporate simulated user feedback to iterate on the solution
- Present the validated prototype with clear metrics on its potential business value

## Key Takeaways

- A working prototype in days is more valuable than a perfect design document that takes weeks
- User feedback on a real prototype reveals insights that no amount of planning can predict
- The goal of prototyping is to validate value, not to build production software; resist the urge to over-engineer
- Knowing when to stop iterating on a prototype and either commit to production or abandon the approach is a critical skill
- The transition from prototype to production should be planned from the start, even if the prototype itself is disposable
