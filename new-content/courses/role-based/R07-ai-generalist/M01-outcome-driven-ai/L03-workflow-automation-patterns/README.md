# L03: Workflow Automation Patterns

## Overview

Learn how to identify automation opportunities within existing business processes and design AI-powered workflows that integrate with current business tools. This lesson covers common automation patterns, integration strategies, and measuring the impact of deployed automations.

## Topics Covered

- Identifying automation opportunities in existing business processes
- Designing AI-powered workflows for real business scenarios
- Integration patterns with existing business tools (CRMs, ERPs, communication platforms)
- Measuring automation impact with before/after metrics
- Handling exceptions and edge cases in automated workflows
- Balancing automation with human oversight

## Lab: Designing and Implementing an AI-Powered Workflow Automation

Design and implement an end-to-end AI-powered workflow automation for a realistic business process, including integration with existing tools and measurement of business impact.

### Lab Objectives

- Identify and document a business process suitable for AI-powered automation
- Design the automated workflow with clear inputs, outputs, and decision points
- Implement the automation with integration to at least one business tool
- Measure and report the business impact (time saved, error reduction, throughput increase)

## Key Takeaways

- The best automation targets are repetitive, high-volume processes with clear rules and predictable inputs
- Integration with existing tools is more important than building new interfaces; meet users where they already work
- Exception handling is what separates prototype automations from production-ready ones
- Measuring impact requires establishing baseline metrics before automation and tracking improvements consistently
- Human oversight should be built into automated workflows, especially during the initial deployment phase
