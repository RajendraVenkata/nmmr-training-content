# L03: Safety Tuning & Alignment

## Overview

Learn how to apply safety fine-tuning approaches and alignment techniques to reduce harmful model behaviors. This lesson covers red-teaming strategies for identifying vulnerabilities, safety-specific training methods, and frameworks for measuring safety improvements across model iterations.

## Topics Covered

- Safety fine-tuning approaches and their goals
- Alignment techniques for reducing harmful outputs
- Red-teaming strategies for identifying model vulnerabilities
- Measuring safety improvements with quantitative metrics
- Balancing safety with model helpfulness and capability
- Responsible disclosure and safety evaluation frameworks

## Key Takeaways

- Safety tuning is an iterative process; no single round of fine-tuning eliminates all harmful behaviors
- Red-teaming should cover diverse attack vectors including jailbreaking, prompt injection, and adversarial inputs
- Over-optimization for safety can lead to models that refuse benign requests; balance is critical
- Safety metrics should be tracked alongside capability metrics to detect trade-offs
- Alignment is not a one-time activity but an ongoing practice throughout the model lifecycle
