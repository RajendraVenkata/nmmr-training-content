# L02: Overcommunication & Visibility

## Overview

This lesson explains why silence is the most dangerous habit for a new hire and how to implement a daily communication framework that keeps you visible to your team and manager. You will learn the specific practices that prevent you from becoming invisible during the critical onboarding period.

## Topics Covered

- Why silence is dangerous: the assumptions managers make when they don't hear from you
- The daily update framework: structure, timing, and channel selection
- Sharing progress: what to include and how to frame it
- Sharing blockers: how to ask for help without appearing incompetent
- Being visible to your team and manager without being disruptive
- Adjusting your communication cadence based on team norms
- Remote and hybrid work visibility strategies

## Key Takeaways

- When a manager does not hear from a new hire, they assume the worst -- overcommunication eliminates this risk entirely
- A simple daily update (what I did, what I plan to do, where I'm blocked) takes five minutes and dramatically changes how you are perceived
- Sharing blockers early is a sign of maturity, not weakness -- managers want to know before a delay becomes a crisis
- Visibility is especially critical for remote and hybrid workers who lack the passive visibility of being physically present
- The goal is not to flood channels with messages but to provide consistent, structured updates that build confidence in your progress
