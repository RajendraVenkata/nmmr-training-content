# L03: Documentation & Win Tracking

## Overview

This lesson covers two habits that pay dividends throughout your career: documenting what you learn and tracking your wins. You will learn how to maintain a running accomplishment log, document processes for your team, and use this evidence to prepare for performance reviews and combat imposter syndrome.

## Topics Covered

- Documenting processes and lessons learned for your team
- Writing onboarding guides and runbooks as a new hire advantage
- Keeping a running log of accomplishments and contributions
- Quantifying your impact: turning tasks into measurable achievements
- Preparing for performance reviews with evidence-based self-assessments
- Combating imposter syndrome with documented proof of your growth
- Using documentation as a tool for team contribution and visibility

## Key Takeaways

- New hires are uniquely positioned to write documentation because they see gaps and confusion that veterans have become blind to
- A running accomplishment log prevents the common problem of forgetting your contributions when review season arrives
- Quantifying impact (time saved, bugs prevented, processes improved) transforms routine work into compelling evidence for promotions
- Imposter syndrome thrives in the absence of evidence -- a documented track record of wins is the most effective antidote
- The habit of documentation compounds over time, building both your reputation as a team contributor and your confidence in your own growth
