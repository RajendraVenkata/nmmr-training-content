# L01: Mindset Shift & Understanding

## Overview

This lesson addresses the most common mistake new hires make: rushing to prove themselves instead of taking time to understand. You will learn how to spend your first two weeks gathering context, asking the right questions, and learning the unwritten rules that govern team dynamics and success.

## Topics Covered

- The understanding-over-proving-yourself mindset in Weeks 1-2
- Why rushing to deliver can backfire in a new role
- Asking the right questions: what to ask, whom to ask, and when
- Gathering unwritten context: team culture, decision-making norms, and political dynamics
- Learning team priorities and how success is measured
- Identifying your manager's expectations and communication preferences
- Building a 30-60-90 day learning plan

## Key Takeaways

- The urge to prove yourself immediately is natural but counterproductive -- understanding the landscape first leads to better contributions later
- Asking thoughtful questions signals intelligence and engagement, not ignorance
- Unwritten context (who makes decisions, what has been tried before, which projects are politically sensitive) is as important as technical knowledge
- Understanding how your team measures success ensures your early contributions align with what actually matters
- A structured 30-60-90 day plan gives you clear milestones and prevents the anxiety of directionless onboarding
