# M04: Your First 90 Days

## Overview

This module prepares you for the critical first 90 days in a new AI role, where habits and impressions are formed that shape your entire tenure. You will learn the right mindset for onboarding, how to communicate effectively with your team, how to document your progress, and how to build strategic relationships that accelerate your growth.

## Prerequisites

- Completion of M01-M03 or equivalent career preparation
- An accepted job offer or imminent start date (recommended but not required)
- Openness to feedback and self-reflection

## Lessons

| Lesson | Name | Has Lab? | Key Topics |
|--------|------|----------|------------|
| L01 | Mindset Shift & Understanding | No | Understanding over proving yourself (Weeks 1-2), asking the right questions, gathering unwritten context, learning team priorities and success metrics |
| L02 | Overcommunication & Visibility | No | Why silence is dangerous, daily update framework, sharing progress and blockers, being visible to your team and manager |
| L03 | Documentation & Win Tracking | No | Documenting processes and lessons learned, keeping a running log of accomplishments, preparing for performance reviews, combating imposter syndrome |
| L04 | Strategic Relationship Building | No | Relationships beyond the immediate team, proactive one-on-ones with managers, reaching out to senior leaders, building trust through "grunt work", demonstrating curiosity/coachability/resourcefulness |

## Key Tools

- Note-taking tools for daily updates and win tracking
- Calendar management for proactive one-on-ones
- Documentation platforms (Notion, Confluence, or team wiki)
- Communication tools (Slack, Teams) for visibility practices

## Learning Outcomes

- Adopt an understanding-first mindset during the onboarding period instead of rushing to prove yourself
- Implement a daily update framework that keeps your manager and team informed of your progress and blockers
- Maintain a running accomplishment log that feeds directly into performance reviews and promotion cases
- Build strategic relationships beyond your immediate team, including with senior leaders and cross-functional peers
- Recognize and combat imposter syndrome using documented evidence of your contributions
- Demonstrate curiosity, coachability, and resourcefulness -- the three traits that managers value most in new hires
