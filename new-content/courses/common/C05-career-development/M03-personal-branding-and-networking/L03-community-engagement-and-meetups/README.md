# L03: Community Engagement & Meetups

## Overview

This lesson covers how to engage with AI communities both online and in person to build genuine professional relationships. You will learn to participate in meetups and conferences, contribute to open-source projects, and engage in Discord and Slack communities in ways that build trust with peers and decision-makers.

## Topics Covered

- In-person meetups and conferences: how to find, attend, and get value from them
- Building trust with decision-makers through authentic interaction
- Open-source contributions as a networking and skill-building strategy
- Discord and Slack community participation: adding value without self-promotion
- Moving from online connections to meaningful professional relationships
- Giving talks and presentations at meetups to build visibility
- The long game: how community engagement compounds over months and years

## Key Takeaways

- In-person meetups create trust faster than any online interaction because they allow for genuine, unscripted conversation
- Open-source contributions demonstrate collaborative skills and technical competence simultaneously to a wide audience
- The most effective community participants focus on helping others and sharing knowledge rather than promoting themselves
- Building relationships with decision-makers happens naturally when you show up consistently and contribute genuinely
- Community engagement is a long-term investment -- the relationships you build today may lead to opportunities months or years later
