# L04: Trade-off Analysis & System Design

## Overview

This lesson prepares you for system design interviews specific to AI applications. You will learn to analyze latency, cost, and quality trade-offs, apply common system design patterns for AI architectures, and practice justifying your design decisions in a structured, interview-ready format.

## Topics Covered

- Latency vs. cost vs. quality trade-offs in AI systems
- When to choose real-time inference vs. batch processing
- Model selection trade-offs: accuracy, speed, and cost per token
- System design interview patterns for AI (RAG pipelines, agent orchestration, inference serving)
- Architecture decision justification: explaining why, not just what
- Scaling considerations for AI workloads
- Mock interview practice: walking through a system design problem end-to-end

## Key Takeaways

- Every AI system design involves a three-way trade-off between latency, cost, and quality -- interviewers want to see you reason about all three
- There is no single correct answer in system design interviews; the quality of your reasoning matters more than the specific architecture
- Justifying your decisions with concrete rationale (expected traffic, cost constraints, latency SLAs) demonstrates senior-level thinking
- Common AI system design patterns (RAG, agent orchestration, model routing) appear frequently and are worth practicing thoroughly
- Mock interviews with peers or mentors are the most effective way to build fluency in system design communication
