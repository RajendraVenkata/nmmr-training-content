# L03: The Storytelling Framework

## Overview

This lesson teaches you a structured framework for presenting your AI projects and technical decisions in interviews. You will learn the Problem-Data-Methods-Failures-Wins-Impact structure that helps you communicate your work clearly to both engineering and business audiences.

## Topics Covered

- The storytelling structure: Problem, Data, Methods, What Failed, What Worked, Business Impact
- Framing the problem in terms the interviewer cares about
- Describing your data sources, quality, and limitations honestly
- Explaining method selection and why you chose one approach over alternatives
- Talking about failures: what didn't work and what you learned
- Highlighting what worked and how you validated success
- Quantifying business impact with metrics that matter
- Adapting your story for engineers vs. business stakeholders

## Key Takeaways

- A structured narrative makes your experience memorable and demonstrates clear thinking under pressure
- Including what failed shows intellectual honesty and a growth mindset -- interviewers value this more than a perfect success story
- Quantified business impact (revenue, cost savings, time reduction) is the single strongest element you can include in any project story
- Engineers want to hear about technical trade-offs and design decisions; business stakeholders want outcomes and ROI
- Practicing your stories out loud until they are smooth and concise is as important as the technical content itself
